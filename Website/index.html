What is HTML?

HTML, or HyperText Markup Language, is the backbone of every web page. It provides the structure and framework for content on the internet. Think of it like the skeleton of a webpage, defining headings, paragraphs, images, links, and more.


Learn More

What is CSS?

CSS, or Cascading Style Sheets, is the artist of the web. While HTML provides the structure, CSS adds style and visual appeal. It allows you to control the layout, colors, fonts, and more, turning a plain webpage into a beautifully designed experience.


Learn More

What is JavaScript?

Functions:
Your Webpage's Superpowers

• Functions are like mini-programs that perform specific tasks.
• They help you make your webpage interactive by enabling actions like saying "Hello!" or doing complex calculations.

Variables:
Remember and Recall

• Variables are memory boxes that store information.
• They allow your webpage to remember things, like a person's name or how many times a button is clicked.

Events:
Making Your Webpage Come Alive

• Events are like strings that connect different parts of your webpage.
• They enable your webpage to respond to user actions, such as clicking a button or moving the mouse, bringing interactivity and life to your site.
